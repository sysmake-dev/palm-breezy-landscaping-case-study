# Palm Breezy Landscaping -- Case Study

**Type:** Concept / Spec Project

**Client:** Local landscaping and lawn care company

**Role:** Design | Frontend Development | Information Architecture

## OVERVIEW

This case study explores the design and development of a mobile-first website for a local landscaping and lawn care business, called Palm Breezy Landscaping, serving residential customers.

The goal is to provide homeowners with a clear and trustworthy place to quickly understand the services offered, determine whether the company serves their area, and easily request a quote.

The case study focuses on clarity, accessibility, and fast mobile performance for customers searching for landscaping services on their phones.

## PROBLEM

Many small landscaping businesses rely heavily on word-of-mouth, yard signs, or social media pages insead of having a dedicated website.

As a result, potential customers often struggle to quickly find reliable information about services, availability, and how to request work. This can effect reputation, which is imperative in landscaping.

Common issues include:

- No clear list of services offered.
- Customers unsure whether the company services their neighborhood.
- Difficulty requesting quotes or contacting the business.
- Information scattered across social media platforms.
- Poor mobile usability.

A simple, well-structured website that provides a central location for this information while helping the business appear more professional and trustworthy would solve these common business challenges.

## CHALLENGES

Lanscaping businesses present a few practical challenges when designing a website. Some of these challenges include:

- Customers often browse quickly while comparing multiple companies.
- Many users will access the site from mobile devices.
- Visitors may be unfamiliar with landscaping terminology.
- The site must build trust quickly to encourage quote requests.

The design must communicate services clearly while keeping the interface simple and easy to navigate.

## CONSTRAINTS

The case study was designed with realistic small-business constraints in mind. These constraints are:

- Mobile-first usage.
- Simple navigation.
- Fast loading speeds.
- Limited technical resources.
- Easy future updates.
- Clear service descriptions.
- Simple quote / contact process.

The site must prioritize clarity and usability over complex design or features.

## DESIGN APPROACH

The design process begins with sketches of the general layout and flow of the site, low-fidelity wireframes to establish layout and information hierarchy, and then full implementation of the site, content, and design.

Key priorities include:

- Clear service categories.
- Large tap targets for mobile users.
- Simple quote request access.
- Strong contrast for outdoor readability.
- Trust-building visual elements such as service descriptions and imagery.

The goal is to help visitors quickly understand what the company offers and how to get started.

## DEVELOPMENT APPROACH

The site is built using semantic HTML, CSS, and lightweight JavaScript, focusing on accessibility, performance, maintainability, and simple content structure. No external frameworks are required.

## OUTCOME

The final result is a clean, mobile-optimized website that allows visitors to view landscaping services, confirm service areas, see examples of work, request a quote quickly, and contact the business easily.

The site provides a clear and professional online presence that supports customer trust and increases the likelihood of new service inquiries.
