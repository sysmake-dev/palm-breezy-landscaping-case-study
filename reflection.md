# Palm Breezy Landscaping -- Reflection

## REFLECTION

Palm Breezy Landscaping was a significant step forward from my first case study, both in scope and execution.

Unlike El Camino Grill, which was a one-page site focused on quick access to essential information, this project required a multi-page structure designed to build trust, clearly communicate services, and encourage user action through quote requests.

## WHAT WENT WELL

The planning process was the strongest part of this project. By defining the services, site structure, homepage content, and navigation flow, before writing any code, the development process became straightforward. The wireframe translated almost directly into the final design, which allowed for faster implementation and fewer revisions.

The mobile-first approach also worked well. Navigation is simple, consistent, and easy to use, especially compared to many local landscaping websites. This directly supports usability and accessibility, which were core priorities.

The design itself achieved the intended goal. There is clear service presentation, strong visual hierarchy, and a clean and professional layout. The site feels trustworthy and easy to use, which is critical for service-based businesses.

## WHAT I LEARNED

This project reinforced the importance of designing for the business, not just for aesthetics.

A landscaping company requires trust, clarity, visible results, and easy contact points. This is very different form a food truck website, even though both are small local businesses.

## KEY TAKEAWAYS

- Strong planning reduces friciton during development
- Different businesses require different design approaches
- Simplicity and clarity are more effective than complexity
- Mobile-first design is essential for local service businesses
- A clean, professional website is often enough to stand out locally

## CLOSING

This case study demonstrates my ability to design and build a multi-page website for a local service business with a focus on usability, accessibility, and real-world business needs.
